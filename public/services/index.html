DMLS & SLM Printing

Our Direct Metal Laser Sintering and Selective Laser Melting systems use high-powered lasers to fuse metal powder layer by layer, producing fully dense parts with mechanical properties comparable to wrought material. Industrial platforms with build volumes up to 400 × 400 × 400 mm process titanium, stainless steel, Inconel, aluminum, cobalt chrome, and tool steels. Parts achieve densities exceeding 99.5% with surface finishes as fine as Ra 6–10 µm as-built.

Rapid Prototyping

Get functional metal prototypes in as little as 48 hours. Unlike traditional prototyping methods that require tooling and fixtures, metal 3D printing builds parts directly from your CAD file — test form, fit, and function with the actual production material. Iterate designs rapidly without the cost and lead time of CNC machining or investment casting setups. Ideal for design validation, functional testing, and small-batch production runs.

Post-Processing & Finishing

Raw DMLS parts require secondary operations to reach final specifications. In-house capabilities include: stress relief and HIP (Hot Isostatic Pressing) heat treatment, CNC machining for critical tolerances to ±0.025 mm, EDM for intricate internal features, surface grinding, bead blasting (Ra 1.6–3.2 µm), electropolishing (Ra 0.4–0.8 µm), and anodizing for colour-coding and corrosion protection.

DfAM Consulting

Design for Additive Manufacturing is key to unlocking the full potential of metal 3D printing. Engineering review covers: part orientation, support minimisation, topology optimisation, lattice infill for weight reduction, internal channel design for conformal cooling or fluid flow, and assembly consolidation. We help convert multi-part assemblies into single printed components — reducing assembly time, fastener count, and total cost.
